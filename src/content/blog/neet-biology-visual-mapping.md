NEET Biology covers a massive volume of content, from cell pathways to complex plant structures and genetics. Many students spend hours highlighted every line in their textbook, only to find they cannot remember specific details during tests.

Textbook highlighting is a passive study habit. To succeed in NEET, you need active recall.

## The Power of Visual Mapping

Visual mapping translates linear text sentences into visual graphs of causes and effects. By drawing node connections, you engage your brain in organizing information actively.

Here is how to create biology concept maps:

- **Create a Core Topic Node:** Write the main topic (e.g., *Photosynthesis: Light Reaction*) in the center of your page.
- **Draw Major Branches:** Branch out into key stages (e.g., *Photosystem II, Electron Transport, Photosystem I*).
- **Add Process Connectors:** Use verbs as connector lines (e.g., *splits water to release oxygen*).
- **Incorporate Colors:** Use blue for enzymes, red for energy molecules ($ATP$, $NADPH$), and green for structures.

## Why Visual Maps Work

1. **Combats Overwhelm:** It condenses 30 pages of text into a single, structured visual sheet.
2. **Promotes Rapid Review:** Before exams, scanning a visual graph takes 5 minutes compared to re-reading chapters.
3. **Improves Question Troubleshooting:** NEET questions often test cross-topic connections. Visual mapping helps your brain link ideas across chapters.
